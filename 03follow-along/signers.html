Who were the Signers

Famous Signers

Thomas Jefferson
Jefferson was born April 13, 1743, on his fathers plantation of Shadwell located along the Rivanna River near the Blue Ridge Mountains of colonial Virginia.1 His father Peter Jefferson was a successful planter and surveyor, and his mother, Jane Randolph, was a member of one of Virginias most distinguished families. When Jefferson was fourteen, his father died, and he inherited a sizeable estate of approximately 5,000 acres. That inheritance included the house at Shadwell, but Jefferson dreamed of living on a mountain.
John Adams
Before becoming President in 1797, John Adams built his reputation as a blunt-speaking man of independent mind. A fervent patriot and brilliant intellectual, Adams served as a delegate from Massachusetts to the Continental Congress between 1774 and 1777, as a diplomat in Europe from 1778 to 1788, and as vice president during the Washington administration.
